What you read off it

  • The shape, not the number. A line that flattens high = sticky. A line that falls off a cliff = people try it once and vanish.
  • X = days since their first trip action (created or joined). Y = % still opening the app that day.
  • The cliff = Goal 3. Wherever the steep drop is (here D0→D3) is exactly where you're losing people.
  • Toggle Host vs Joiner. Same chart, flip the audience. Hosts (dashed) staying higher than joiners would tell you joiners are the leak.
One decision: "active" = opened the app that day. Simple and honest.

What you read off it

  • Ranked by adoption. Top = the feature people love. Bottom = the feature you built that nobody touches.
  • "Ever used", yes/no. "22% ever opened the personal gear list" — one clean signal per feature.
  • Frequency is deliberately out. How often they use it is a v2 question; for now we just want hits vs misses.
  • Same toggle. Hosts and joiners use different things — admin updates matter to hosts, messages to everyone.
  • Time window picker. Today, last 7/30 days, all-time, or any custom range. The bars recompute to "who used it within that window" — so you can watch a feature heat up or fade after a change.
The payoff: the bottom of this list is your "cut it or fix it" shortlist.

What you read off it

  • A warm streak = a living trip. The crew keeps showing up. A row that fades to white is slowly dying; an abrupt stop is dead.
  • "Active" = engaged with THIS trip that day (opened it, messaged, touched gear) — not just any app open. That's the careful part: app-opens would call a dead trip alive.
  • Sorted so dying trips sink down + a one-word Health tag, so you can scan, sort, and act (or fire a re-engagement nudge).
  • The pattern jumps out: here the big crews (top) hold heat, the small ones (bottom) fade fast — your "bigger = stickier" hunch, made visible.
Why a heatmap, not a curve: one averaged line hides the dying trips inside the average. This shows every trip's trajectory at once.

Deliberately left out of step one

Trip size · time-to-trip · people type become filters you layer on later, not charts now. They're real hypotheses — but 15 views on day one means you look at none. Two charts, one toggle, then we add depth once these earn their keep.
