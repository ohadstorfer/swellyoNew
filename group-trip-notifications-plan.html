Group Trip Notifications

A simple, smart plan — keep members informed, pull dormant users back, never spam.

Scope: group_trips Push + In-app feed Goal: inform + re-engage

1 Principles

Five rules every notification must pass.

  • P1Push only if it's timely AND useful right now. Everything else lives in the feed.
  • P2Always land on an action. Every tap opens the exact screen to do the next thing.
  • P3One person, one action = one notification. Batch the rest into a digest.
  • P4Respect quiet hours. Hold non-urgent pushes from 9pm to 8am local.
  • P5Never repeat. If they already saw it in the feed, don't push it too.

2 The golden rule: push vs feed

Decide the channel before anything else.

  • Push when it needs a decision, is time-sensitive, or is about them directly.
    e.g. you're approved · trip starts tomorrow · someone asked to join your trip
  • Feed only when it's nice-to-know group activity that can wait.
    e.g. someone claimed gear · gear list was edited · a 4th member joined
Quick test: "If they never open the app, does this matter?"  Yes → push.  No → feed.

When NOT to send (anti-spam guardrails)

  • G1Don't re-engage anyone active in the last 24 hours.
  • G2Max 1 re-engagement push per user every 3 days.
  • G3Back off after 2 ignored re-engagement pushes in a row.
  • G4Never during quiet hours (9pm–8am local).
  • G5Never if the user muted that trip or turned off re-engagement.

5 Smart rules — the engine behind it

Cross-cutting logic that keeps the system calm. Tagged for this round: NOW ship with push · LATER phase 2/3 · SKIP dropped for now.

  • NOWSR8Priority order. Decisions > reminders > group activity > re-engagement. High priority skips the batch.Foundational — it's how we decide what pushes instantly vs waits. Just a priority field; the other rules lean on it.
  • NOWSR4Dedup vs feed. If they already read it in the bell, cancel the pending push.Kills the most obvious annoyance — being pinged for something you just saw. High value, low cost.
  • NOWSR6Mute per trip. Respect the existing mute; add a per-trip toggle.Mute already partly exists for chats — cheap to honour it for trip pushes too.
  • NOWSR5Collapse. One trip = one live push — newest replaces the older one.Nearly free via Expo collapseId / Android thread id. Keeps the lock screen tidy.
  • LATERSR1Batch. Group rapid events — 3 joins in an hour become one "3 people joined".Needs the push queue + a batching window. Worth doing, but after the backbone is solid.
  • LATERSR3Quiet hours. Queue non-urgent pushes and release them at 8am local.Needs a per-user timezone we may not store yet. Phase it once TZ is sorted.
  • LATERSR2Frequency cap. Max ~3 pushes per day per user across all their trips.Needs a sent-push log to count against — the queue's sent rows can serve as that later.
  • SKIPSR7Granular opt-out. Separate switches: reminders · group activity · re-engagement.Needs a preferences screen (Phase 3). Out of scope for this round.
