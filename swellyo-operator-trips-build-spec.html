The shape of it

What we're actually building

Swellyo today is a free peer-to-peer trips product — a user hosts a trip, others request to join, no money moves. We're adding a second, parallel product for professional operators: real payments, a structured traveller onboarding, and a management dashboard.

The one-line success criterion

One design-partner operator runs one real trip end-to-end inside Swellyo — creates it, their travellers onboard and pay and upload documents, and they manage the whole thing to completion without falling back to spreadsheets and WhatsApp. Depth over breadth. One operator, done properly, beats five half-served.

How it relates to the existing product. Peer trips and their live users are completely unaffected — no schema, RLS or behaviour change to anything running today. Operator trips reuse the chat system and can optionally appear in the explore feed, but otherwise run on their own rails. Peer hosts don't get operator features in v1.
Locked — but reviewable

Decisions we've taken

These came out of the spec interview. Each one includes the reasoning so you can judge whether you still agree. If any of these flip, the sections below change with them.

Operator identity

Locked
A normal Swellyo account with an is_operator flag we set by hand.
No self-serve signup, no application queue, no verification UI in v1. Eyal onboards each operator personally. Why: at this stage the constraint is finding good operators, not scaling access to them — building signup infrastructure for six people is wasted work.

Payments

Locked
Real money collection, both rails at launch — Stripe Connect for US/international, per-operator gateway integration for Israeli operators.
Not payment-tracking. Travellers actually pay inside Swellyo. Why both: tashlumim and Bit only exist on the Israeli rail, so an Israeli operator without it can't sell the way they sell today. See the payments section.

Israeli commission

Locked
Operator remits manually against the totals we track.
Their gateway account is theirs, so we can't deduct at source without an Israeli entity. Because payment happens inside Swellyo, we still see the amounts — the tracking is real even though the collection isn't automatic.

Discovery

Locked
Per-trip visibility toggle: private (link only) or listed in explore.
Operators distribute their own link to their own audience by default. Listing is the growth lever they opt into, trip by trip. Listed operator trips need a badge and a price so users can tell them from peer trips.

New traveller entry

Locked
Full Swellyo onboarding first, then trip onboarding.
One consistent funnel. Flagged risk: this is the likeliest place in the whole flow to lose an operator's paying customer — someone who booked a surf trip and is now being asked to build a social profile. Instrument it and be ready to revisit.

Capacity

Locked
A spot is secured when the deposit is paid — not before.
Prevents ghost reservations blocking real buyers. Someone mid-onboarding can lose their spot to someone who pays first, so the UI has to say that plainly rather than surprise them.

Waivers

Locked
Operator uploads the waiver, traveller reads it and taps "I agree". We record name, timestamp and waiver version.
Legally reasonable for this use case and trivial to build. Drawn signatures or DocuSign are available later if an operator's insurer demands it.

Staff

Locked
Display-only profiles — name, photo, role, bio.
No accounts, no permissions, no access. Staff exist so travellers can see who's guiding them. Giving guides real trip access is a v2 conversation.

Communication

Locked
Reuse group chat and admin updates, plus automated deadline reminders and a 1:1 operator↔traveller DM.
Automated reminders are what actually removes the chasing — without them the operator is still the one nagging. The DM exists because payment and medical questions don't belong in a group thread.

Desktop

Locked
Read and review only — payments, trip info, exports. All real management on mobile.
Keeps the web surface tiny at a time when web isn't a platform we maintain. Document review and exports are genuinely painful on a phone, so those are the things that earn a desktop view.

Architecture

Locked
Separate data model, bridged to the existing chat system. Live tables untouched.
Full reasoning in the architecture section — the short version is that bookings behave nothing like join-requests, and the live trip tables are more entangled than they look.
Not decided

Open questions — do not invent answers

Each of these blocks a specific part of the build. Two need a conversation with the design-partner operators; one needs research and probably a lawyer; one is ours to decide.

Pricing model

Blocker

Flat price per person, or room types (shared vs private), or options plus add-ons (transfers, board rental, extra nights)? Real surf trips almost never have one flat price. Blocks: payment amounts, the booking table shape, capacity semantics.

Ask the design-partner operators

Payment timing

Blocker

Where does payment sit in the onboarding? Deposit during onboarding with the balance as a later open task, full payment upfront, or operator-configurable per trip? Note the tension already flagged in the operator guide: operators often need cash upfront for suppliers, which fights against holding funds until after the trip. Blocks: onboarding step order, booking state machine.

Ask the design-partner operators

Refund & chargeback liability

Blocker

We charge the card, so Stripe disputes land on us — and if an operator refuses to refund, we're stuck in the middle. Candidate structure to validate: operator is contractually liable for delivery and refunds, chargebacks debited from their connected account (debit_negative_balances: true), refunds only ever executed on operator instruction, deposit explicitly non-refundable. Do not finalise the money-holding design until this is settled.

Research + design partners + likely a lawyer

Document retention

Open

Two candidates, both acceptable: (a) private bucket with auto-delete N days after the trip ends — lower liability; or (b) private bucket, kept, so returning travellers never re-upload their passport. Option (b) is a real product benefit and a real liability increase. Access control is identical either way.

Architecture

Separate model, bridged to chat

The decision that most affects how this gets built, and the one worth understanding properly.

Why not just add a flag to group_trips

Two reasons, both concrete:

1. The live tables are more entangled than they look. A single SECURITY DEFINER function, is_trip_host(), gates six tables — participants, join requests, commitment requests, gear items, gear requests and admin updates. It was hand-patched to production as recently as 8 July 2026 with an explicit "do not db push" warning. Bolting a payment and document lifecycle onto that means every operator change has a blast radius covering six features that have nothing to do with money.

2. A booking is not a join-request. It holds real inventory, its state is gated on payment, and it has to prevent double-selling. That isn't an incremental extension of request-to-join — it's a different lifecycle wearing the same word.

The lucky part. Chat is already loosely coupled — messagingService.createGroupConversation stores trip_id inside conversations.metadata (JSONB), not as a foreign key to group_trips.id. Operator trips can create group conversations through exactly the same path with zero migration.

Data model

New tables

Shapes firm up once the pricing model and payment timing are answered — those two decide the booking and payment columns. Everything else is stable.

operator_trips operator_id, trip content, capacity, visibility (private | listed), payment config, onboarding requirements config, waiver (text/file + version), staff (jsonb), status operator_trip_bookings trip_id, traveller_id, state, chosen price option, amounts state: onboarding_started → deposit_paid (spot secured) → fully_paid → completed | cancelled operator_trip_payment_events ← immutable ledger booking_id, source (stripe | gateway | manual), type, amount, currency, external_ref, occurred_at Webhooks and manual marks APPEND events. Payment status is DERIVED, never mutated directly. operator_trip_traveler_documents booking_id, type (passport | insurance | visa | flights), storage_path, uploaded_at, deleted_at, reclaimed_at operator_trip_tasks trip_id, definition, deadline + per-traveller completion state

The one non-obvious design choice

The payment ledger is append-only. Webhooks never mutate a booking's payment status directly — they append an event, and status is computed from the event history. This is what makes the dashboard's money numbers trustworthy and replayable when a webhook arrives twice, out of order, or late. It's the single easiest thing to get wrong and the most expensive to fix afterwards.

Scope

Features, by side

Straight from the three feature sketches, cleaned up and de-duplicated.

Traveller

  • Extended onboarding on top of normal Swellyo onboarding
  • Passport upload
  • Waiver — read and agree
  • Medical form: allergies, dietary, injuries, regular medication
  • Payment (position TBD)
  • Deadline-gated, skippable: insurance, visa, flight tickets
  • Overview: staff highlighting, improved "what's included"
  • Plan: open tasks, uploaded items, "my medical card" + edit, helpful links
  • Carried over: chat, gear, admin updates, profiles, notifications

Operator

  • Trip creation — current wizard plus:
  • Set onboarding requirements per trip
  • Set open tasks and deadlines
  • Set payment requirement timing
  • Fill in staff info
  • Configure notifications — self and customers
  • Set visibility and capacity
  • Simple management: a dedicated edit space, simpler than the creation wizard
  • Communication: chat, admin updates, gear lists
  • Mobile only

Dashboard

  • Trips list → select trip or create new
  • Pay status: paid in full, total in $ and ₪, open payments, deposits paid, tashlumim total
  • Documents: completion counts (14/15 passports), visas, insurance, flights + view all
  • Medical flags: allergies, food prefs, injuries + view all
  • Surf demographics: levels, board types, age ranges, nationalities
  • Each with its own page + export all
  • Per traveller: profile, documents (view/export/delete+reclaim), medical (view only)
  • Actions: message, remove from trip, send reminder
"Delete + reclaim" means the operator deletes a traveller's uploaded document and the system automatically re-opens it as a task for that traveller, with a notification — e.g. because the passport scan was unreadable. It's a delete plus a re-request, not just a delete.
Payments

Two rails, one dashboard

Correction worth knowing before you plan anything

Stripe can do deposits. Stripe cannot do tashlumim. A deposit plus balance is straightforward on Stripe (two charges, or auth-then-capture). Israeli interest-free instalments run over SHVA and require Israeli domestic acquiring, which a US entity can't get on Stripe — confirmed across several earlier research passes. Splitit stacks instalments onto Stripe but adds cost rather than removing it, so it isn't a substitute. This is precisely why both rails are in v1.

US / international — Stripe Connect

Live account approved, Connect enabled, test connected account exists. Direction already agreed from earlier research: Express accounts, destination charges, ~12% commission, funds held until after the trip. The remaining build-time decisions there (charge type, loss liability) now interlock with the refund-liability question above.

Israel — per-operator gateway integration

The operator connects their own gateway account (API key / webhooks) so payments report amount and payer back into Swellyo automatically. This is what makes the ₪, deposit and tashlumim numbers on the dashboard real rather than trust-based. We're not in the money flow; commission is remitted manually against tracked totals. Consequence to accept: this only works for operators on a gateway we've integrated.

Dashboard money view. Both rails shown together, in dollars and shekels — total collected, paid in full, deposits paid, outstanding, tashlumim.
Sequencing

Build order

Ordered so that the parts blocked on operator conversations come after the parts that aren't.

01

Operator flag + trip creation

The is_operator flag, operator trip creation, visibility toggle, capacity. No payments yet.

02

Traveller onboarding

Documents, waiver, medical form, tasks with deadlines. The biggest single chunk of product value and it needs no payment decisions.

03

Dashboard — mobile

Trip snapshot, per-traveller view, exports.

04

Payment rail #1 + the ledger

Whichever rail the first design partner actually needs. The append-only ledger lands here.

05

Reminders + 1:1 DM

Automated deadline reminders and the operator↔traveller thread.

06

Payment rail #2

The other rail, once the first is proven in a real trip.

07

Desktop read-only dashboard

Payments, info, exports. Review surface only.

Risks and what we do about them

RiskMitigation
Feed fragmentationTwo trip types in one feed — solved with the read-only UNION view rather than merging write paths.
Two parallel permission systemsStrict naming and documentation discipline, no shared foreign keys between the two models.
Payment webhook correctnessAppend-only ledger with derived status — handles duplicate, out-of-order and late webhooks.
Onboarding drop-offFull Swellyo onboarding before trip onboarding is the likeliest place to lose an operator's paying customer. Instrument it from day one and be willing to reverse the decision.
Sensitive dataPassports and medical records are the most sensitive data Swellyo will ever hold. Private bucket, RLS limited to the traveller and that trip's operator, short-lived signed URLs, medical view-only with no export, retention policy shown before upload.
Gateway lock-in per operatorOnly operators on an integrated gateway get automatic tracking. Accept it, and pick the first gateway based on real signed operators rather than research preference.
