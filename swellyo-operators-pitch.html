The part nobody talks about

Sound familiar?

  • A WhatsApp group of fifteen people asking the same five questions on repeat.
  • A spreadsheet where you track who paid the deposit - and who you still have to chase.
  • A folder of passport photos, half of them blurry, none of them named properly.
  • Insurance you've asked for three times and still don't have from two people.
  • Flight details arriving as screenshots, one at a time, over four weeks.
  • And the night before departure, you're still not certain who has a bad shoulder.
15 travellers × 4 documents = 60 files

Collected by hand, one message at a time, on every single trip you run - before a single wave gets surfed.

None of this is why you started running surf trips. So stop doing it.

What changes

Three things you stop worrying about.

01

The chasing stops.

You decide what each trip needs - passport, waiver, insurance, visa, flights, medical form - and when it's due. Swellyo collects it and reminds people automatically. You open your phone and see 14 of 15 passports in, exactly who's missing, and a one-tap nudge to send them.

No more "did you send me your passport?" at 11pm.
02

Every payment in one view.

Deposits taken, balances outstanding, who's paid in full, who's on instalments - in shekels and dollars, on one screen. Travellers pay inside Swellyo, so the number is right without you reconciling anything. Israeli operators keep their own payment processor and their own rates.

The spreadsheet stops being the source of truth.
03

You land knowing your group.

Allergies, dietary needs, injuries and medication. Surf levels, board types, ages, nationalities. The whole picture of who's coming, before you get on the plane - so you pick the right breaks, brief the right people, and nothing catches you out on day one.

Better guiding, and one less safety risk to carry.
