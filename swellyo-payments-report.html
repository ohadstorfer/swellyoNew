Swellyo Payments — Cheat Sheet

Stripe Connect · US + Israel · Delaware LLC · pre-launch
The plan: Stripe Connect Express. 12% host commission. Swellyo pays Stripe fees. Cards + ACH at launch. Add Splitit later for Israel.
Bottom line @ $50k/mo: Stripe costs ~$1,615 · Swellyo nets ~$4,325.

🔧 Services to use

  • Stripe Connect Express use — payment system for marketplaces. Stripe holds money, pays hosts.
  • Stripe Radar use — fraud detection. $0.07/transaction. Cuts chargebacks ~17%.
  • 3DS2 use — 2-factor at checkout. Moves fraud loss to the bank, not you.
  • ACH Direct Debit use — US bank transfer. $5 max fee vs $58 card. Save ~$53/trip.
  • Stripe Financial Connections use — verifies US bank before ACH charge. Kills failure rate.
  • Apple Pay / Google Pay / Link use — faster checkout. Same fees, free to turn on.
  • Mercury Treasury use — business bank with 4.5% interest on idle money. Sweep weekly.
  • Stripe Atlas use — $500 service that incorporates your Delaware LLC.
  • Tax1099.com use — files 1042-S tax forms for foreign hosts. ~$500/year.
  • Splitit later — lets Israelis pay in monthly installments on existing card. Works with Stripe.
  • Wise (for host payouts) don't — routing host money via your bank = money transmitter law trouble.
  • Stripe Connect Standard don't — removes your control of when hosts get paid. Bad for travel.
  • Bit / PayBox / native Tashlumim skip — need an Israeli company to access these.

🇮🇱 Israel — key facts

  • Tashlumim = Israeli installment payments. Cultural default for anything over $270. Not available via Stripe.
  • Israeli cards on Stripe cost +1.5% extra (cross-border fee). Unavoidable.
  • Always charge in USD, never ILS — avoids extra 1% FX fee.
  • Israeli hosts: open their own Stripe account, get paid in ILS or USD.
  • If Israel grows big → register Israeli entity + use Tranzila (Israeli payment processor) to unlock Bit + Tashlumim.

💸 Fee split — $1,500 trip example

  • Traveler pays $1,500
  • Stripe takes $43.80 (Swellyo absorbs)
  • Host gets $1,320 (88%)
  • Swellyo gross $180 → net $136 after Stripe
  • If ACH: Stripe takes only $5 → Swellyo net $175

📋 Week 1 — must do blockers

  • Incorporate Delaware LLC via Stripe Atlas ($500)
  • Open Stripe Connect Express account
  • Turn on W-9 / W-8BEN collection (tax forms hosts must fill)
  • Hire lawyer for Terms of Service + Host Agreement + Privacy Policy ($1.5–3.5k)
  • Sign DPAs with Supabase, Stripe, PostHog (one click each — required by GDPR)
  • Fill PCI SAQ-A form (15 min self-survey, required for taking cards)

⚡ Week 2 — turn on quick wins

  • Stripe Radar ON
  • 3DS2 ON
  • ACH Direct Debit + Financial Connections
  • Apple Pay / Google Pay / Link
  • Set debit_negative_balances: true on every host (Stripe pulls from host's bank if chargeback)
  • Add $50–100 non-refundable booking deposit
  • Charge everything in USD only
  • Open Mercury account, sweep idle balance weekly

🇮🇱 Before first Israeli host blocker

  • Confirm W-8BEN is enforced (Israel host gets 0% withholding via US-Israel treaty)
  • Without W-8BEN → you legally owe IRS 30% of every payout
  • Set up 1042-S filing with Tax1099.com or a CPA

🚨 Never do

  • Never route host money through Swellyo's bank account — triggers money transmitter laws
  • Never offer free instant payouts — costs 1%
  • Never frame listings as "accommodation + surf package" — triggers lodging tax in 30 states. Frame as "surf experience" instead.

⏸ Skip for now

  • Israeli VAT registration — law not yet enforced
  • EU representative — Stripe handles PSD2/SCA automatically
  • CCPA (California) — below threshold
  • Chargeflow / Justt — too small to justify
  • Money Transmitter License — Stripe's license covers you
