  • Free plan: $0/month. Same transaction fees as Pro.
  • Pro plan: $79/month. Adds custom branding, eSignatures, auto-billing, CRM integrations, lead capture. 30-day money-back.
  • Pro does NOT reduce transaction fees — it's features-only. There is no volume tier that lowers processing fees.

"0% booking fee" claim — what it actually means

WeTravel has no separate marketplace commission. The "0%" or "1%" headline refers to ACH being WeTravel's pitched method. Card transactions always cost 2.9% or 3.9% — that fee can be passed to the traveler or absorbed by the operator, but it's never zero.

🏦 Payout fees verified

  • Local bank payout (US, EU, AU): Free, 1–3 business days.
  • Instant payout (US, debit card on file required): 1.5% of payout amount.
  • International wire payout: WeTravel's docs cite $15, $25, and $30 across different pages. unclear — real number not consistently published.
  • FX conversion within WeTravel wallet: Claimed "free" but the daily rate used is internal and not disclosed. User reports describe spread of ~1–2% vs bank rates. estimate

🌍 Currency support verified

  • Trips can be priced in: 36 currencies.
  • Operator payout wallet supports 20 currencies: USD, EUR, GBP, AED, AUD, BRL, CAD, CHF, CZK, DKK, HKD, HUF, JPY, MXN, NOK, NZD, PLN, SEK, SGD, TRY.
  • ILS (Israeli Shekel) is NOT supported as a payout currency. Israeli operators must receive USD or EUR and wire to Israel themselves.
  • Payments received in unsupported currencies auto-convert to USD at WeTravel's "daily rate" (spread undisclosed).

Key takeaways from the table
  • The card processing fee (2.9% or 3.9%) is the only fee WeTravel takes from the operator. There is no separate marketplace commission.
  • Whether the traveler or the operator sees the fee depends entirely on a setting the operator picks during trip creation.
  • Israeli operator paid via international wire loses an estimated ~$80 of $1,500 (~5.3%) to card fee + wire + FX spread.
  • Israeli travelers always hit the 3.9% AMEX/international tier plus their bank's USD→ILS conversion markup.

↩️ Refunds & chargebacks verified

  • Card processing fee is NOT refunded on cancellations. The 2.9% / 3.9% stays with WeTravel + the card network and is deducted from the operator's balance.
  • ACH refunds: free — the 1% is refunded.
  • Default refund policy: non-refundable. Cancellation terms are 100% operator-defined.
  • Chargeback fee: no published flat fee (Stripe charges $15; WeTravel's number is undisclosed). unclear
  • Chargeback liability: operator bears the full disputed amount + "administrative fees" per the help center.
  • WeTravel does not fight disputes on the operator's behalf.

🚨 Account freeze risk verified via reviews

Multiple operator complaints across Trustpilot, Capterra, and PissedConsumer document the same pattern: WeTravel can freeze an operator's entire balance for "compliance review" with no advance notice, no SLA, and no phone support.

  • One operator: "Trips in progress, payments due to hotels and guides, and WeTravel held ALL funds during a 'compliance review' that lasted weeks with no explanation."
  • Another: "Frozen after 8 months, ~€50,000 in client money, zero contact from the company."
  • Another reported filing an FTC complaint after WeTravel blocked transactions mid-trip (October 2024).

⏱ Payout timing verified

  • Default: WeTravel pays out 1–3 business days after each payment clears. They do NOT hold deposits until trip departure by default.
  • Operators CAN set up deposit + balance payment plans (e.g., $500 now, $1,000 due 60 days pre-trip). Each scheduled payment is collected from the traveler at its trigger date and paid out to the operator within 1–3 days.
  • This means the popular claim that "WeTravel holds your money for months" is only true for compliance freezes, not by design.

🇮🇱 Israeli traveler / operator specifics

Israeli traveler paying

  • Card hit at 3.9% (international tier) verified
  • USD→ILS conversion handled by the Israeli card issuer, not WeTravel — typically 1.5–3% markup on top. estimate
  • WeTravel does NOT pass installment metadata to Israeli card networks. Native Tashlumim is unavailable. verified
  • WeTravel's own payment-plan feature is just deferred billing — not interest-free installments and not the same as Israeli card-network Tashlumim.

Israeli operator receiving

  • ILS payout: not supported. Operator receives USD or EUR. verified
  • Israel is NOT on WeTravel's blocked-country list — Israeli operators can onboard. verified
  • Every payout to an Israeli bank is an international wire ($15–$30, inconsistently documented). unclear amount
  • FX spread on USD→ILS conversion estimated at 1–2% based on user reports. estimate
  • WeTravel does not withhold tax on operator payouts — that's the operator's responsibility. verified

🔍 Other small print verified

  • No built-in tip/gratuity prompt at checkout. Operators can add upsells (gear rental, transfers, lessons) as add-ons.
  • Insurance at checkout: WeTravel partners with Trawick International and Pattern Insurance. Travelers opt in during booking.
  • Growth Capital: a referral product (launched Nov 2022) where operators can borrow against future bookings via a 3rd-party lender.
  • WeTravel Expense Card: a corporate card for operators with FX markups and international transaction fees (specific numbers not published).

❓ What WeTravel does not clearly publish

  • Exact international wire payout fee ($15, $25, and $30 all appear in different help center pages)
  • FX spread on currency conversion (claimed "free" but rate is internal)
  • Flat chargeback fee amount
  • Default for "pass fee to traveler" on new operator accounts
  • Whether ACH booking confirmation is held until settlement (5–7 days) or confirmed immediately
  • Whether Israeli traveler checkout displays totals in ILS or USD before charge
